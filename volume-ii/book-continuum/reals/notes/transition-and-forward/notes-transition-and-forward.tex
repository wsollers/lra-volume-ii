\begin{remark}[What Completeness Buys]
\label{rem:what-completeness-buys}
The least-upper-bound property promotes the arbitrary closeness available in
$\mathbb{Q}$ to guaranteed limits in $\mathbb{R}$: bounded monotone sequences
converge, Cauchy sequences converge, and suprema exist. The general theory of
these phenomena belongs to analysis; this chapter supplies the complete ordered
field in which they live.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[Parallel Constructions]
\label{rem:parallel-constructions-forward}
Cauchy sequences, nested intervals, and dyadic expansions give alternative
constructions of complete ordered fields. By
\hyperref[thm:uniqueness-of-cof]{Uniqueness of the Complete Ordered Field},
each yields this same $\mathbb{R}$ up to unique isomorphism.
\end{remark}
