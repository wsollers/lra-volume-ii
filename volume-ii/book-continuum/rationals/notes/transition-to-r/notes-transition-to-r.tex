\subsection*{Why the Real Numbers Are Introduced}

\begin{remark}[Why the Real Numbers Are Introduced]
\label{rem:why-real-numbers-are-introduced}
The rational numbers form an ordered field, are dense in themselves, and
satisfy the Archimedean property. These facts make $\mathbb{Q}$ a rich
arithmetic and order structure, but they do not make it complete.

The square-two gap shows the defect precisely: there is a nonempty
bounded-above subset of $\mathbb{Q}$ with no supremum in $\mathbb{Q}$. The real
numbers are introduced as a completion of $\mathbb{Q}$, so that bounded-above
sets acquire suprema and Cauchy sequences acquire limits.
\end{remark}
