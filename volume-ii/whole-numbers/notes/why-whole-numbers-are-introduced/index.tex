\section{Why Whole Numbers Are Introduced}
\label{sec:why-whole-numbers-are-introduced}

\begin{topicbox}{Adjoining an Additive Identity}
\begin{exposition}
The natural-number system developed in the preceding chapter is one-based:
its distinguished first element is $1$. This is sufficient for counting and
recursive arithmetic, but addition on $\mathbb{N}$ has no additive identity
inside $\mathbb{N}$. The whole numbers are introduced by adjoining a new
element before constructing the integers.
\end{exposition}
\end{topicbox}
