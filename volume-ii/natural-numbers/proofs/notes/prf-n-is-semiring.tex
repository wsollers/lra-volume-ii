\begin{theorem*}[$\mathbb{N}$ Is a Semiring]
Let $(P,S,1)$ be a Peano system. The system $(P,+,\cdot)$ is a semiring:
$(P,+)$ is a commutative semigroup, $(P,\cdot,1)$ is a commutative monoid,
and multiplication distributes over addition on both sides.
\end{theorem*}
