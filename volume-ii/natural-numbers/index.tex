\noindent\textbf{How we got here.}
Volume~I established the Peano framework and the legitimacy of recursive
definitions on $\mathbb{N}$. This chapter begins from that foundation and
develops the arithmetic of the natural numbers.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter develops.}
The chapter begins with Peano systems and the induction principle, then
defines addition and multiplication by recursion, proves the corresponding
algebraic and order laws, and finally identifies the structural form of
$\mathbb{N}$.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Where this leads.}
The natural numbers support counting and recursive arithmetic, but subtraction
is not an internal operation on $\mathbb{N}$. That limitation is what leads
to the construction of the integers.
\end{tcolorbox}
