\begin{theorem*}[Uniqueness of Binary Iterator Operations]
Let $(P,S,1)$ be a Peano system, let $A$ be a set, let $W$ be a set, and
suppose each $a\in A$ determines iterator data $(W,c_a,g_a)$ for $(P,S,1)$.
If $F,G:A\times P\to W$ satisfy the same base and successor clauses for every
$a\in A$, then $F=G$.
\end{theorem*}
