\noindent\textbf{How we got here.}
The preceding chapters developed the number systems in ordinary mathematical
notation, beginning with Peano systems and then constructing
$\mathbb{N}$, $\mathbb{Z}$, $\mathbb{Q}$, and $\mathbb{R}$. This final chapter
records how part of that development can be mirrored in Lean 4.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter develops.}
This chapter gives a quick orientation to Lean 4 syntax used to formalize the
Peano-system material, especially the way predicates and subsets are
represented as functions into `Prop`.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Where this leads.}
Lean is not used here as a second beginning for the volume. It is a compact
formal record of the preceding Peano-system development, and an orientation
for reading the companion formalization alongside the notes.
