\noindent\textbf{How we got here.}
The embedding chapter explained why the number systems can be compared inside
one coherent chain.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter develops.}
This chapter introduces the geometric language of number lines and the basic
notation for intervals: open intervals, closed intervals, half-open intervals,
rays, and the whole line. The goal is notation and order intuition, not metric
topology.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Where this leads.}
Once interval notation is fixed, the next chapter studies $\mathbb{R}$ as a
metric line: distance, neighborhoods, open and closed sets, and compactness.
\end{tcolorbox}
