\noindent\textbf{How we got here.}
The previous chapters constructed $\mathbb{N}$, $\mathbb{W}$, $\mathbb{Z}$, $\mathbb{Q}$, and $\mathbb{R}$ as separate systems, each with its own elements, operations, and order.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter develops.}
This chapter defines embeddings as injective, structure-preserving, order-reflecting maps and assembles the canonical chain
\[
\mathbb{N}\hookrightarrow\mathbb{W}\hookrightarrow\mathbb{Z}
\hookrightarrow\mathbb{Q}\hookrightarrow\mathbb{R}.
\]
The construction chapters establish the local bridge facts; this chapter explains their common form, records the canonical maps, and proves their compatibility as one coherent system.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Where this leads.}
After identification along these embeddings the systems become nested substructures of $\mathbb{R}$. The next chapter introduces the visual language of number lines and intervals.
