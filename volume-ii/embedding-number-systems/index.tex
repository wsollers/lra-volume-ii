\noindent\textbf{How we got here.}
The previous chapters constructed $\mathbb{N}$, $\mathbb{W}$, $\mathbb{Z}$, $\mathbb{Q}$, and $\mathbb{R}$ as separate systems, each with its own elements, operations, and order.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{What this chapter develops.}
This chapter defines embeddings as injective, structure-preserving, order-reflecting maps, fixes the canonical embedding at each step of the chain $\mathbb{N}\hookrightarrow\mathbb{W}\hookrightarrow\mathbb{Z}\hookrightarrow\mathbb{Q}\hookrightarrow\mathbb{R}$, proves their compatibility, and introduces number lines and intervals, ending with the characterization of intervals as the order-convex sets.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Where this leads.}
After identification along these embeddings the systems become nested substructures of $\mathbb{R}$, which the next chapter studies as an ordered metric space.
\end{tcolorbox}
