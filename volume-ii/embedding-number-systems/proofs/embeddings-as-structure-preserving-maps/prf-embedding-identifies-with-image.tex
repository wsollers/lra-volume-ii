\begin{theorem*}[Embeddings Identify a System with Its Image]
Let $\varphi:A\to B$ be an embedding of ordered arithmetic systems. Then $\varphi:A\to\varphi(A)$ is a bijective structure-preserving order isomorphism, and $A$ may be identified with $\varphi(A)\subseteq B$.
\end{theorem*}

\begin{remark*}[Proof structure]
Surjectivity onto the image is immediate from the definition of $\varphi(A)$; injectivity is given. The structure-preservation and order-reflection clauses of an embedding give the homomorphism and order-isomorphism conditions on the corestriction. The inverse is structure-preserving because $\varphi$ is. Conclude that $\varphi$ is an isomorphism onto its image.
\end{remark*}
