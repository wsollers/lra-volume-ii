\section{Compatibility of Addition, Multiplication, and Order}
\label{sec:embedding-compatibility-arithmetic-order}

\begin{topicbox}{Compatibility of Arithmetic and Order}
\begin{exposition}
The important point is compatibility. Each embedding allows arithmetic done
in the smaller system to agree with arithmetic done after moving into the
larger system. The same holds for order: if two elements are ordered in the
smaller system, their images have the same order relation in the larger
system. These facts justify the familiar practice of moving expressions
between number systems without changing their value or their order
relationships.
\end{exposition}
\end{topicbox}
