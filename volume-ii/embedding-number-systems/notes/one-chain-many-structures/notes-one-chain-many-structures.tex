\section{Summary: One Chain, Many Structures}
\label{sec:embedding-summary-one-chain-many-structures}

\begin{topicbox}{One Chain, Many Structures}
\begin{exposition}
The chain
\[
\mathbb N \hookrightarrow \mathbb W \hookrightarrow
\mathbb Z \hookrightarrow \mathbb Q \hookrightarrow \mathbb R
\]
is not merely a chain of names. It records a sequence of structure-preserving
identifications. Each new system solves a limitation of the previous one:
\(\mathbb W\) adds zero, \(\mathbb Z\) adds additive inverses, \(\mathbb Q\)
adds division by nonzero elements, and \(\mathbb R\) adds the completeness
needed for real analysis.
\end{exposition}

\begin{exposition}
After the embeddings are established, the ordinary notation becomes harmless:
the same numeral may be used across several systems because the maps preserve
the structure that matters. The foundational distinction remains in the
background, while the working notation regains the simplicity used in
analysis.
\end{exposition}
\end{topicbox}
