\section{Number Lines}
\label{sec:number-lines}

\begin{topicbox}{Number Lines}
\begin{exposition}
A number line is a visual representation of an ordered number system. For
\(\mathbb W\), the points begin at zero and proceed discretely to the right.
For \(\mathbb Z\), the line extends discretely in both directions. For
\(\mathbb Q\), there are rational points between any two distinct rational
points, but the rational line still has gaps from the point of view of limits
and completeness. For \(\mathbb R\), the order is complete in the sense
developed earlier in the real-number chapter.
\end{exposition}

\begin{exposition}
The embedding chain gives a disciplined interpretation of the usual picture.
The whole-number line sits inside the integer line, the integer line sits
inside the rational line, and the rational line sits inside the real line.
Each step preserves the visible order and the arithmetic structure already
available at the previous step.
\end{exposition}
\end{topicbox}
